What's all this about, anyway?

There's a word-puzzle game available on all mobile phone networks called WordScapes. It's got various offshoots, all under the same license, I think, but they're all in a format where there's a sort of crossword layout at the top of the screen and a circle of letters at the bottom. It's up to the players to fill in the blanks using the letters on the wheel.

I get very frustrated when I get to the last word and can't work it out. I doubt I'm alone in this. Therefore, I thought I'd write an app to help me cheat, and share that app with the world. I don't think it's costing the games company anything, so my conscience is clear.

Is this any help at Wordle?

Not really. It can help you see permutations, but you can only play Wordle once a day. This app exists just to get your from one screen to another out of a virtually infinite number of screens. It's worthwhile to bend the rules a little to cross the bridge from one screen to another.

Wordle has only one screen at a time. Once you solve it that's you done for twenty-four hours. If you're running your Wordle through this app you're really as well not to play Wordle at all.

Why isn't there a spellchecker?

Because I'm too cheap to pay for the storage a dictionary would take. Sorry.
